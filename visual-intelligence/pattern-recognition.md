# Pattern Recognition

Pattern recognition is not the same as meaning.

A pattern begins with recurrence. Something appears again: a shape, a gesture, a color relation, a composition, a sequence, a motif.

Context determines whether recurrence matters. The same repeated form can signal style, ritual, coincidence, convention, propaganda, memory, or nothing at all. Repetition without context is only accumulation.

Significance enters when a pattern changes interpretation or action. A meaningful pattern is one that helps explain what is being shown, why it appears, and what kind of world produced it.

Machines are strong at detecting recurrence across large sets. Humans are stronger at asking whether the recurrence means devotion, fashion, warning, grief, authority, parody, or desire.

Visual intelligence requires both distinctions at once: notice the repeated thing, then determine whether it carries weight. Detection is the first step. Meaning is the harder judgment that follows.
