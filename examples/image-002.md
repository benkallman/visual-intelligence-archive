# Interpretation: Recurring Arch Form Across Photographic Series

*This file demonstrates archive-conditioned reading: interpretation shaped by what recurs across a set of images, with explicit attention to what the archive structure itself may be imposing.*

---

## Pass 1: Literal Description

A series of twelve black-and-white photographs. Each image contains an arched doorway or window as the dominant structural element. The arches vary in scale and elaboration: some are plain stone, some are ornately carved, two include partial figures standing within or before the arch.

All images share a consistent framing strategy: the arch is centered, the photographer's position is perpendicular to the plane of the arch. In eleven of twelve images, the area beyond the arch is lighter than the area in front — the arch frames a passage toward light.

No captions are present on the images. The archive folder label reads: "threshold studies – undated."

---

## Pass 2: Reflective Interpretation

**Recurrence finding:** The arch-toward-light composition recurs in 11 of 12 images. This is statistically significant within the set. The recurrence is real.

**Archive-conditioned reading:** The recurrence may reflect the photographer's deliberate thematic focus — a series *about* thresholds. Alternatively, it may reflect selection bias: a later archivist curated these images from a larger set because they share this feature. These two explanations produce different interpretive conclusions. Both must be held open.

**Symbolic candidate:** The arch-as-threshold, when light is consistently beyond it, is a recurring symbolic form in many traditions (passage, transition, entry, death, liberation, sacred approach). Its presence as a compositional choice across 11 images strengthens its candidacy. *Confidence: medium-high within this series. Basis: intentional compositional repetition.*

**Archive context caution:** The label "threshold studies" was applied by a human archivist. It may reflect the photographer's intent or the archivist's interpretation. Treating this label as documentary evidence of meaning would conflate the labeler's interpretation with the image content. *Label noted, not trusted as ground truth.*

**Interpretive claims:**

The consistent framing (arch centered, light beyond) is a deliberate compositional choice, not incidental. The photographer chose this relation repeatedly. The series is organized around a structural grammar. *Confidence: high. Basis: 11/12 compositional repetition.*

The arch-toward-light motif functions as a symbolic candidate for threshold, passage, or transition within this series. The meaning is portable across the images in the set. *Confidence: medium. Basis: compositional intent + cross-traditional symbolic recurrence of this form.*

**Rejected claims:**

- "The series documents a specific building or location" — insufficient evidence. Multiple arch types appear; no location markers are present. *Rejected.*
- "The photographer intended a meditation on death" — this is one possible reading within the threshold range but cannot be confirmed without external evidence (statements, context, period). *Reduced to candidate status.*
- "The arch recurrence means this is religious photography" — religious architecture appears but so do secular forms. Arch recurrence in secular contexts is common. The recurrence supports symbolic candidacy, not categorical identification. *Rejected.*

---

**Witness note:** Archive label ("threshold studies") noted but not treated as authoritative. The framing choice is the primary evidence for interpretive claims. Human witness with knowledge of the photographer's context and the archive's curation history is needed to resolve the intentionality question.
