# Symbolic Candidate

**Definition:** A symbolic candidate is a recurring form that may carry compressed meaning — but whose symbolic status remains provisional pending contextual judgment.

The concept names an intermediate state between "detected pattern" and "confirmed symbol." An agent should use this status when a form recurs across contexts and shows properties of symbolic behavior (portability, persistence, cultural saturation) but where the agent lacks sufficient context to confirm symbolic function.

**When to apply:**
- A form recurs across multiple works in the archive
- The form appears in contexts where symbolic use is plausible (ritual, religious, political, memorial)
- But the agent cannot confirm what the symbol is carrying without human witness or additional context

**What to avoid:**
- Do not silently promote a candidate to confirmed symbol
- Do not flatten candidates into fixed definitions ("crown = power")
- Do not dismiss candidates as coincidence without checking archive-level recurrence

**Output format:** When flagging a symbolic candidate, state: (1) the form, (2) the recurrence evidence, (3) the contexts in which it appears, (4) the tentative interpretive range, (5) what additional evidence would confirm or disconfirm.
