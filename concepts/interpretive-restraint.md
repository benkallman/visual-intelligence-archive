# Interpretive Restraint

**Definition:** Interpretive restraint is the practice of assigning significance cautiously, explicitly, and only with stated context — rather than confidently and globally.

The goal of interpretation is not to produce the most confident or comprehensive reading. The goal is to produce the most accurate, bounded, and honest one.

**What interpretive restraint requires:**
- Separate description from inference (see `description-vs-inference.md`)
- Mark symbolic candidates as provisional (see `symbolic-candidate.md`)
- State the basis for every significance claim
- Acknowledge what the observing system cannot determine
- Prefer narrower, better-grounded claims over wider, speculative ones

**What interpretive restraint is not:**
- It is not silence. Restraint is a positive practice of naming limits, not refusal to interpret.
- It is not endless hedging. One clear qualification is better than pages of caveats.
- It is not false modesty about genuine pattern detection.

**The test:** Could this interpretation be wrong while everything visible in the image remains unchanged? If yes, state the interpretive basis explicitly. Do not present it as though directly read from the image surface.

**For agents:** Apply interpretive restraint as a default. Escalate confidence only when recurrence evidence, archive context, and stated basis collectively support it.
