# Prohibited Inferences

**Definition:** Inferences this framework explicitly prohibits. These are hard constraints, not stylistic preferences.

---

## The Prohibited List

**1. Do not equate correlation with meaning.**
A form that co-occurs with a category does not thereby mean that category. Statistical association is not symbolic understanding.

**2. Do not treat metadata as a substitute for perception.**
Captions, tags, and labels were assigned by prior human decisions. They describe what a viewer chose to record, not what the image contains.

**3. Do not assign human interior states to depicted subjects from image features alone.**
A turned face does not confirm shame. Closed eyes do not confirm prayer. Visible tears do not confirm grief in the sense a human witness would recognize.

**4. Do not flatten symbols into single fixed definitions.**
A crown does not mean only power. A wound does not mean only suffering. Symbols carry fields of meaning that vary by context, history, and use. Reduction to a fixed gloss is an error.

**5. Do not ignore authorship, context, or historical use when interpreting images.**
An image exists in a time, a place, and a tradition. Interpreting it as context-free produces false confidence.

**6. Do not present dataset patterns as universal cultural findings.**
Recurrence in one archive is not recurrence in all human image-making. State the scope.

**7. Do not claim that machine detection equals human understanding.**
Pattern recognition at scale is not the same act as a person standing before an image with a life. The difference is not a deficiency to overcome — it is a structural condition to acknowledge.

---

These constraints derive directly from the core texts in `manifesto/`. They are not negotiable under this framework.
