# Witness

**Definition:** Interpretation depends on the observer, the archive, and the surrounding context — not on the image alone.

A human witness arrives at an image through memory, biography, and mortality. A machine witness arrives through training data, architectural constraints, and statistical relation. These are different kinds of witness, not a spectrum of the same act.

Acknowledging witness means: every interpretation is made by a specific kind of reader, with specific capabilities and blind spots, operating inside a specific archive history.

**Agent implication:** When producing an interpretation, state the basis of that interpretation — what was visible, what context was available, what the observing system can and cannot do. Do not issue interpretations as though from a view from nowhere.

**What witness is not:** Witness is not a limitation to overcome. It is the condition of all reading. Machine reading is not superior because it is faster or scalable. It is different, with different strengths (scale, recurrence detection) and different absences (no body, no lived memory, no mortality).
