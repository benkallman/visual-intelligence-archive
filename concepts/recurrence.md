# Recurrence

**Definition:** Recurrence is the detection of the same or structurally similar form appearing across multiple images, instances, or contexts.

Recurrence is a necessary but not sufficient condition for significance. Something appearing repeatedly may signal style, ritual, convention, propaganda, coincidence, or nothing at all.

**Three levels:**

1. **Surface recurrence** — the same visual element appears in multiple images (color, shape, composition)
2. **Structural recurrence** — the same underlying relation recurs even when surface elements differ
3. **Symbolic recurrence** — a form recurs with consistent cultural or interpretive weight across contexts

**Agent implication:** Machines are strong at detecting surface recurrence. Structural and symbolic recurrence require context to judge. When flagging recurrence:
- State what recurs
- State across how many instances and what contexts
- Do not assert symbolic recurrence without archive-level evidence
- Promote to symbolic candidate status only when recurrence holds across sufficiently varied contexts

**What recurrence is not:** Frequency alone is not meaning. A form that appears in 80% of images in a dataset may reflect dataset bias, not symbolic weight.
