# Archive Context

**Definition:** Archive context is the dataset, curation history, institutional framing, and selection biases that shape what a machine reading system encounters and how it is trained to respond.

Machine vision is never only about images. It is also about the habits of viewers, the values of curators, and the hidden theology of datasets.

**What archive context includes:**
- What images were collected, by whom, and with what intent
- What labels, captions, or metadata were assigned
- What was excluded or underrepresented
- What cultural frameworks shaped the categorization

**Agent implication:** An agent reading images inside an archive is reading through that archive's prior decisions. Recurrence findings may reflect collection bias rather than cultural significance. Labels may reflect the labeler's assumptions, not neutral description.

**Practical rule:** When making claims based on archive-level patterns, state the archive source and its known limitations. Do not present patterns from a biased or narrow collection as universal findings.

**What archive context is not:** Archive context is not an excuse to avoid interpretation. It is a required component of honest interpretation. The goal is transparency about the frame, not paralysis.
